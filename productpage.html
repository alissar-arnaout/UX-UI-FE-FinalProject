About this Brand

Zara and it’s sister brands are owned by Inditex, a Spanish based clothing company. Originally co-founded by white Spanish business people Amancio Ortega and Rosalia Mera, worth $70.6 billion and $6.1 billion, respectivly, the company is now owned by stakeholders that bring over $23 billion in revenue each year. Over the years Inditex has been under fire regarding their growing pollution consumption and unfair wages for their workers.

Inditex has tried to take steps towards a better future but they seem to fall short every time. In 2016, they created their Join Life range that used recycled materials, but it only accounts for a small percentage and results in not impact. In an attempt to look greener Inditex created the “Close The Loop” program allowing shoppers to return their used garments either in-store or through the post to be recycled. However in 2019, Index pledged to make all collections from 100% sustaniable faberics and 80% of their engery consumed by it’s headquarters, factories and stores would be renewable by 2025. Unfortantly the company has still not disclosed the number of resourses that go into production.
